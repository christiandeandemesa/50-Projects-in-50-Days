Frequently Asked Questions

Do people have free will?

It depends on how you define free will.

How can we know if we have knowldege?

Knowledge is defined as a justified true belief, although there is evidence that opposes that.

What, if any, are universal human rights?

As stated here, 1) security rights that protect ourselves from harm, 2) due process rights that guarantee fair trials and punishments, 3) libery rights that protect individual freedoms (e.g. beliefs), 4) political rights that protect political actions (e.g. voting), 5) equality rights that guarantee all persons are treated equally, and 6) social rights that promise basic necessities provided by society (e.g. education).

Is beauty subjective or objective?

Technically objective in that things that are beautiful are symmetrical, but there are differing opinions.

Which duties does a legitimate government owe to its citizens, and vice versa?

John Dewey argued here that all organized people (e.g. families) are a form of government designed to regulate its members. Thus, citizens are required to follow the government's laws since they are, on average, ignorant.
